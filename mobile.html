Archive of Collective Memory

Please use the text box below to make your submission to the Archive of Collective Memory.
All submissions are anonymous and untracked.
All text will be transcribed into music and played throughout the Shanties, forming our "collective memory"


The Archive of Collective Memory is an interactive public art installation as part of the 2023 Art Shanty Projects in Mpls, Minnesota.
The Archive offers an opportunity to visitors to reflect on the nature of representation and the relationship between individual and collective memory.
From within its hexagonal wooden structure, the Archive plays audio transcribed from audience member's text inputs into music.
Each weekend, the Archive will collect these submissions and add them to the Archive.
This first weekend, the Archive will playback a transcription of an translated work by Maurice Halbwachs (1877–1945). In the work, Halbwachs' describes his conception of "collective memory".
On the final day of the Art Shanties, the Archive will play back its contents – the accumulated music from all submissions to the archive - offering a space for reflection on what has been collectively represented and stored within the Archive.
